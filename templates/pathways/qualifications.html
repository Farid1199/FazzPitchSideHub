Overview

Whether you want to coach, manage, scout, or work in football development, there’s a qualification pathway for you. The FA delivers qualifications through England Football Learning, their online learning portal. Most courses combine online modules with practical assessments.

This page brings together every key qualification relevant to non-league football in one place — coaching licences from Level 1 to the UEFA A Licence, scouting and Talent ID courses, safeguarding certificates, and first aid training.

FA Coaching Licences

The FA coaching pathway takes you from complete beginner to elite level. Each licence builds on the last. Here’s the full ladder:

FA Playmaker

Free online course. Perfect starting point for parents and complete beginners. Learn basic session planning and how to make football fun for children.

FREE

Online • ~3 hrs

FA Level 1 in Coaching Football

Introduction to coaching. Covers session planning, basic techniques, and working with small groups. Suitable for anyone 16+. Often the entry requirement for coaching at grassroots clubs.

£150–£200

30–40 hrs

FA Level 2 in Coaching Football

Develop your coaching skills further. Learn to plan and deliver sessions with clear outcomes. Required to manage most non-league teams from Step 5 downward. Covers tactical principles and player development.

£300–£400

80–120 hrs

UEFA C Licence

First UEFA-recognised qualification. Designed for coaches working with 11v11 teams. Strong emphasis on match-day coaching, team shape, and in-game management. Commonly held by Step 3–5 managers.

£500–£700

120+ hrs

UEFA B Licence

Advanced coaching qualification. Focuses on long-term player development, advanced tactics, and leadership. Required by many Step 1–2 clubs. Often held by academy coaches and experienced managers.

£800–£1,200

180+ hrs

UEFA A Licence

Elite coaching qualification. For first-team managers at semi-professional and professional level. Covers high-performance environments, team management, and strategic planning. Held by EFL and National League managers.

£3,000+

240+ hrs

Tip: You don’t need to rush. Many successful non-league managers spent years at Level 2 before progressing. Focus on gaining experience alongside your qualifications.

Scouting & Talent Identification

FA Talent Identification Course

The primary scouting qualification. Learn a structured approach to evaluating players across technical, tactical, physical and psychological attributes. Suitable for scouts, coaches and club recruitment staff.

£150–£250

Weekend course

Advanced Talent Identification

For experienced scouts. Covers data-driven scouting, video analysis, advanced report-writing, and working with club recruitment departments. Often required for academy scouting roles.

£300–£500

Multi-day course

Safeguarding & DBS Checks

Anyone working with children or vulnerable adults in football must hold valid safeguarding qualifications. These are mandatory at every level of the game.

FA Safeguarding Children Workshop

Mandatory for all coaches, scouts and volunteers working with anyone under 18. Covers recognition of abuse, reporting procedures, and best practice.

£20–£30 3–4 hours • Renew every 3 years

DBS Check (Enhanced)

Criminal records check required for anyone in “regulated activity” with children. Applied for through the FA’s CRB/DBS process via your County FA.

Free (via FA) 2–8 weeks processing

Important: You cannot coach, manage, or scout at any FA-affiliated club without a valid DBS check if your role involves contact with children. Clubs face sanctions if this is not enforced.

First Aid

FA Emergency First Aid in Football

Mandatory for all qualified coaches. Covers treating common football injuries, CPR, and when to call emergency services. Practical and theory-based.

£50–£80 6–8 hours • Renew every 3 years

FA Advanced First Aid

For physiotherapists, sports therapists and medical staff at higher non-league clubs. Covers advanced trauma, concussion protocols and match-day medical requirements.

£150–£250 16+ hours

Costs & Funding Options

Qualifications aren’t cheap, but there are ways to reduce the cost or get funding:

County FA Bursaries

Many County FAs offer bursaries or subsidised course places for coaches from underrepresented groups or working in deprived areas.

Club Sponsorship

Some non-league clubs will fund qualifications for their coaches. Ask your club committee — it’s an investment in the club’s future.

Football Foundation Grants

The Football Foundation sometimes funds coach education as part of wider community projects. Check their website for current programmes.

England Football Learning Portal

Free and low-cost CPD modules available online. Build your knowledge between formal qualifications with micro-courses.
