Overview

Non-league football is the beating heart of the English game. Thousands of players compete across Steps 1–7 and local Sunday leagues every week. Unlike the professional game, non-league is accessible to almost anyone with talent, dedication, and the right approach.

Whether you’re a teenager hoping to break into semi-professional football or an adult looking to get back into the game, this pathway will guide you through the key steps, what clubs look for, how to find trials, and how to build a football profile that gets noticed.

Trials & Opportunities

Open Trials

Most non-league clubs (Step 3–7) hold open trial days, especially during pre-season (May–July). These are open to anyone — just turn up, register, and play.

Invitation Trials

Some clubs run invite-only trials after watching players. Having a strong online profile and highlight reel increases your chances of being noticed by scouts.

Where to Find Trials

Club websites, social media, and platforms like FAZZ PitchsideHub aggregate trial listings so you can find them in one place.

What Clubs Look For

Fitness, attitude, technical ability, and positional awareness. Communication and teamwork matter just as much as individual skill at trial level.

Player Development

Talent alone is not enough. Clubs at every level of the pyramid value players who are committed to improving. Here are the key areas to focus on:

Fitness

Cardiovascular endurance, agility and strength are essential at higher steps.

Game Intelligence

Reading the game, positioning, and decision-making separate good from great players.

Attitude & Teamwork

Coaches consistently rate attitude, coachability and communication as top traits.

Building Your Football CV

A football CV is your calling card. It tells clubs and scouts who you are before they’ve seen you play. Here’s what to include:

  1. 1.Personal Details — Name, age, position, preferred foot, height.
  2. 2.Playing History — List clubs, teams, and leagues you’ve played in (most recent first).
  3. 3.Key Stats — Goals, assists, appearances — be honest and specific.
  4. 4.Achievements — Cup wins, player-of-the-month awards, representative honours.
  5. 5.Video Highlights — A 2-3 minute YouTube reel of your best moments. Quality over quantity.
  6. 6.References — A previous manager or coach who can vouch for your ability and character.
